PCA (worked example) and Logistic Regression for handwritten digit recognition

Notebooks

In this topic's lab, we consider two topics

  1. Principal Components Analysis (PCA) is a powerful technique for reducing the dimensionality of a feature space while keeping as much as possible of the predictive power of the full feature space. This notebook (right-click and download as Digits/PCA_introduction.ipynb) walks through the procedure and describes how it works by applying it to a very simple example, where the number of dimensions is low enough to be visualised easily.

  2. Logistic Regression was covered in class. This notebook gives students an opportunity to walk through this classification procedure, using the implementation in scikit-learn. The data we choose to classify is the well-known NIST handwriting recognition dataset. This notebook (right-click and download as Digits/LogisticRegression.ipynb) brings together train-test split, cross-validation, classifier performance analysis and ridge regression, so students are encouraged to study it carefully.

  3. Comparing KNN and Logistic Regression with Bangalore EED epilepsy dataset. This dataset contains EEG traces from multiple areas of the scalp, for 1000s of patients, from those without epilepsy to those with different grades of the disease. The notebook computes classification reports, ROC and AUC for 2 classes, which are treated as being with and without epilepsy. This notebook (right-click and download as Epilepsy/KNN_LR.ipynb) combines train-test split, row sampling, use of KNN and LinearRegression, classification reports and ROC/AUC analysis.

The second of these notebooks suggests the following exercise:

  • add dimensionality reduction/PCA from the other notebook.

The third of these notebooks invites students to use SMOTE oversampling to re-balance the partial epilepsy data, and to compare the resulting metrics with those obtained earlier in the notebook, identifying the differences and explaining them.
