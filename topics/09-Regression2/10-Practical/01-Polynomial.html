Analysis

An ordinary least squares (OLS) fit is used. Note that the original feature (x) is transformed to create features which are monomials (powers of x). These polynomial features add less value as their degree increases. Regression metrics are used to show this.

One of the signs of overfitting is when successive beta coefficients become large and oscillate in sign. Therefore, note the plot that shows the range (i.e., max - min) of beta as the degree increases.

Consequently, a regularised linear regression is performed as well. As can be seen, with an appropriate level of regularisation (enough, but not too much), we can use higher degree polynomials to follow the data more closely, while limiting the scope for overfitting (as represented by surious "wiggles" in the model).

Students are invited to compare the two approaches, and to note the advantages and disadvantages of each.
